Most plastic surgery marketing agencies sell traffic — more ads, more impressions, a bigger top of funnel. But at surgical case values, the expensive leak isn't awareness. It's the visitor who researched your practice, almost inquired, and left. Fix that, and the traffic you already have is worth far more.

## What a plastic surgery marketing engagement should deliver

Booked consultations. Not reach, not rankings for their own sake — consultations on the schedule. Everything this practice does ladders up to that one number, through two services:

- **[Conversion optimization](/plastic-surgery-cro/)** on the consult funnel — inquiry forms, procedure pages, financing clarity, gallery UX.
- **[Local SEO](/plastic-surgery-seo/)** — the map pack plus procedure-page authority in your market.

## Where surgical practices actually leak

The funnel runs visitor → inquiry → consultation → surgery, and the money hides in the middle. Website conversion is the cheapest stage to fix — no extra ad spend, and it compounds. If you want the numbers first, the [consultation conversion benchmarks](/blog/plastic-surgery-consultation-conversion-rate/) show where each stage typically bleeds.

## Agency vs. marketing consultant for plastic surgeons

If you have an in-house marketing person and want the roadmap and oversight, that's a consultant relationship. If you want the tests built and run for you each month with accountability for booked consults, that's this. The work is the same discipline either way — the difference is who executes.

## Who should hire a full-service incumbent instead

If you want one vendor to run Google Ads, social, PR, web development, and reputation management as a bundle, hire an established full-service plastic-surgery agency — the Etna Interactive / Studio III tier exists for exactly that, and they're good at it. This practice is the opposite bet: two levers, one senior operator, one accountable test a month. Pick the bet that fits.
