Most plastic surgery marketing sells traffic — more ads, a bigger top of funnel. But at surgical case values, the expensive leak isn't awareness. It's the visitor who researched you, almost inquired, and left.

## 🎯 The owner's mental model

A surgical practice is a funnel, not a megaphone:

**Cases = inquiries × consult rate × close rate × average case value.**

<Callout type="stat">

At surgical case values, a single extra converted consult a month can exceed an entire marketing retainer. That's why conversion — not more top-of-funnel spend — is the lever that pays.

</Callout>

## 📈 Lever one: convert inquiries into booked consultations

The consult funnel runs visitor → inquiry → consultation → surgery, and the money hides in the middle. The specific leaks:

- Inquiry forms that ask too much, too soon
- Procedure pages with no pricing or financing signal
- Before/after galleries buried or gated
- No fast, clear handoff to a coordinator

We run [one controlled test a month](/plastic-surgery-cro/) on the spot that matters most. Start with the [consultation conversion benchmarks](/blog/plastic-surgery-consultation-conversion-rate/) to see where each stage typically bleeds.

## 🔍 Lever two: rank for the procedures your market searches

A patient searching "rhinoplasty" or "breast augmentation" plus your city should find your practice first. That's the [local SEO](/plastic-surgery-seo/) layer:

- The practitioner-vs-practice Google Business Profile question (get it wrong and you split your signal)
- Reviews and RealSelf presence
- Procedure-page authority in your market
