Most plastic surgery marketing sells traffic: more ads, more impressions, a bigger top of funnel.

But at surgical case values, the expensive leak isn't awareness.

It's the visitor who researched your practice, compared your before/afters, almost filled out the form, and left.

This guide starts there, with the funnel, because for a surgical practice that's where the money is.

## <span className="he">🎯</span> The owner's mental model

A surgical practice is a funnel, not a megaphone:

**Cases = inquiries × consult rate × close rate × average case value.**

Each stage is a separate rate and a separate fix.

A practice with brilliant surgeons and a weak inquiry-to-consult rate doesn't have a talent problem.

It has a follow-up problem, and that's a marketing and operations fix, not a clinical one.

<Callout type="stat">

At surgical case values, a single extra converted consult a month can exceed an entire marketing retainer.

That's why conversion, not more top-of-funnel spend, is the lever that pays for a surgical practice.

</Callout>

## <span className="he">📈</span> Lever one: convert inquiries into booked consultations

The consult funnel runs visitor to inquiry to consultation to surgery, and the money hides in the middle.

The specific leaks, in order of how often they cost the most:

- **Inquiry forms that ask too much, too soon.** Long forms lose high-intent patients before they finish.
- **Procedure pages with no pricing or financing signal.** Patients need a sense of investment and [financing](/blog/plastic-surgery-financing-marketing/) before they'll request a consult.
- **Before/after galleries buried or gated.** Your strongest proof, hidden from the people deciding. Used well, [galleries are a conversion asset](/blog/plastic-surgery-before-after-galleries/).
- **No fast handoff to a coordinator.** [Speed-to-lead and a patient coordinator](/blog/patient-coordinator-consult-conversion/) who owns the booking decide whether an inquiry ever becomes a consult.

The fix is [plastic surgery CRO](/plastic-surgery-cro/): one controlled test a month on the spot that matters most, run to significance.

Start with the [consultation conversion benchmarks](/blog/plastic-surgery-consultation-conversion-rate/) to see where each stage typically bleeds, and by how much.

## <span className="he">🔍</span> Lever two: rank for the procedures your market searches

A patient searching "rhinoplasty" or "breast augmentation" plus your city should find your practice first.

That's the [local SEO](/plastic-surgery-seo/) layer, and it has a surgical wrinkle med spas don't face.

- **The practitioner-versus-practice Google Business Profile question.** Multi-surgeon practices have to decide how listings map to individual surgeons versus the practice. Get it wrong and you split your ranking signal.
- **Reviews and RealSelf.** For surgical decisions, [reviews and RealSelf presence](/blog/plastic-surgery-reviews-realself/) carry real weight in both the decision and the rankings.
- **Procedure-page authority.** Your patient-facing procedure pages, structured so Google trusts them as the authority in your market.

Unlike ads, this compounds. The rankings you earn this year keep sending consults next year.

The full walkthrough, including the multi-location and RealSelf specifics, is in [how to rank a plastic surgeon on Google](/blog/how-to-rank-plastic-surgeon-google/).
