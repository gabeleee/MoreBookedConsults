<AtAGlance>

- **Track a short list,** not a cluttered dashboard.
- **The essentials:** conversion rate, cost per consult, LTV vs CAC, retention.
- **Conversion rate has the most leverage;** it multiplies everything.
- **Ignore vanity metrics** unless they convert into booked patients.

</AtAGlance>

Most med spas either track nothing or track everything, and both leave you flying blind.

The fix is a short list of metrics that actually drive decisions, so you know what to change instead of drowning in numbers that don't matter.

## <span className="he">📊</span> The KPIs worth tracking

These are the numbers that tell you what to do next.

- **Conversion rate:** leads and site visitors that become booked consults
- **Cost per booked consult:** ad spend divided by consults, not cost per click
- **[Lifetime value](/blog/medspa-patient-lifetime-value/) vs [acquisition cost](/blog/medspa-customer-acquisition-cost/):** the two halves of profitability
- **Retention and [rebooking rate](/blog/medspa-client-retention/):** how many patients come back
- **Revenue per treatment room or provider:** how productively capacity is used

If a metric doesn't change a decision, it doesn't belong on your dashboard.

## <span className="he">⚡</span> Why conversion rate leads the list

<Callout type="key">

Conversion rate is the metric with the most leverage, because it multiplies every other number.

Lift it and your cost per booking falls, your return on every marketing dollar rises, and none of it requires spending more. That's why [fixing conversion](/medspa-cro/) is almost always the highest-return work available.

</Callout>

A practice tracking conversion rate knows exactly where its funnel leaks. One that isn't is guessing.

## <span className="he">🚫</span> The vanity metrics to ignore

<Callout type="warning">

Followers, raw traffic, impressions, and likes feel like progress but rarely are.

They only matter if they convert into booked, kept, returning patients, so track the conversion downstream of them, not the vanity number itself.

</Callout>

A viral post that books nobody is worth less than a quiet page that converts, and confusing the two is how practices celebrate numbers while the calendar stays empty.

## <span className="he">🎯</span> Measure what you can act on

The point of KPIs isn't a prettier report. It's better decisions.

Keep the list short, make sure you can [track each number honestly](/blog/medspa-attribution-call-tracking/), and review them on a regular cadence so a problem shows up as a trend, not a surprise.

Then let the numbers drive your [budget](/blog/medspa-marketing-budget/) and your priorities, instead of gut feel.
