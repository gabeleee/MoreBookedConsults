- **CPC is the wrong number.** Cost per booked consult is the one that matters.
- **CPC varies widely** by treatment and market; competitive terms run high.
- **Conversion rate drives cost per booking** more than CPC does.
- **Judge cost against lifetime value,** not against the first visit.

</AtAGlance>

Ask "how much do med spa Google Ads cost" and you'll get a cost-per-click number, which is almost useless on its own.

The click is not the goal. The booked consult is, and the price of that depends far more on your site than on the auction.

## <span className="he">💵</span> What clicks actually cost

Cost-per-click for aesthetic treatments swings a lot by market and by term.

Competitive, high-value queries like Botox, CoolSculpting, or laser can run well into double digits per click, while broader or less competitive terms cost less.

But two practices paying the same CPC can have wildly different real costs, because one converts clicks into consults and the other doesn't.

## <span className="he">🎯</span> The only number that matters

<Callout type="key">

Cost per click times clicks equals ad spend. Ad spend divided by booked consults equals your real cost, and that's the number to manage.

A $10 click looks expensive until it books a $4,000 patient. A $3 click looks cheap until you realize it never books at all.

</Callout>

Stop optimizing CPC in isolation and start tracking cost per booked consult, because that's the figure that tells you whether the channel is working.

## <span className="he">⚡</span> Conversion rate is the hidden lever

Here's the part agencies skip: your [conversion rate](/medspa-cro/) moves cost per booking more than your bidding does.

If you pay for clicks and only 3 percent book, you're funding the 97 percent who bounce, so doubling your conversion rate roughly halves your cost per consult with zero change to ad spend.

That's why the highest-return move in paid search usually isn't in the ad account at all. It's on the landing page.

## <span className="he">🧮</span> Judge cost against value, not the first visit

A booked consult that looks expensive can still be a bargain.

Measure it against [patient lifetime value](/blog/medspa-patient-lifetime-value/) and your [true acquisition cost](/blog/medspa-customer-acquisition-cost/), not the margin on a single treatment, and a lot of "too expensive" ad spend turns out to be highly profitable.
