<AtAGlance>

- **Established med spas** commonly spend **7 to 12% of revenue** on marketing.
- **New practices** spend more, often **15 to 20%,** to build awareness.
- **The right number comes from lifetime value,** not a rule of thumb.
- **Watch the definition:** does the budget include ad spend, or just management?

</AtAGlance>

"How much should I spend on marketing?" has a lazy answer (a percentage) and a real answer (whatever keeps your cost per patient well below their lifetime value). Here's both, so you can sanity-check the lazy one against the real one.

## <span className="he">📊</span> Marketing spend by practice stage

Figures below are drawn from aggregated public benchmarks as of 2026 and vary by market and treatment mix.

| Practice stage | Typical marketing spend (% of revenue) |
|---|---|
| Pre-opening / launch | 15 to 20%+ |
| Growth (year 1 to 3) | 10 to 15% |
| Established | 7 to 12% |
| Mature / referral-heavy | 5 to 8% |

New practices spend more because they're buying awareness they don't yet have. As referrals and repeat visits compound, the percentage can fall while revenue rises.

## <span className="he">💡</span> The real way to set a budget

<Callout type="key">

Don't budget from a percentage. Budget from [patient lifetime value](/blog/medspa-patient-lifetime-value/). If a patient is worth $2,000 over their relationship and costs you $200 to acquire, spending *more* to acquire more of them is the obvious move, up to the point where cost per patient approaches value.

</Callout>

That means the two numbers you actually need are [customer acquisition cost](/blog/medspa-customer-acquisition-cost/) and lifetime value. Everything else is a starting guess.

## <span className="he">⚠️</span> The definition trap

A "$2,500/month" budget can mean management only, or management plus ad spend, or everything including creative. Always clarify, because the real all-in number can be double the headline. The [med spa marketing cost](/medspa-marketing-cost/) page breaks down what's typically included.

## <span className="he">📏</span> Track it against results

A budget without [attribution](/blog/medspa-attribution-call-tracking/) is a guess with a decimal point. Tie spend to booked consults and to your [KPIs](/blog/medspa-kpis/), and revisit quarterly.
