- **CAC = total acquisition spend ÷ new patients acquired.**

- **Referrals and organic** cut blended CAC over time.

</AtAGlance>

Customer acquisition cost is the number that quietly decides whether your marketing makes money, and most med spas have never actually calculated theirs.

Once you do, a lot of "expensive" channels turn out to be bargains, and a few "cheap" ones turn out to be losing money.

## <span className="he">🧮</span> How to calculate it

The blended version is simple: total everything you spent to acquire patients in a period, then divide by the new patients you acquired.

Include ad spend, agency fees, and acquisition-tied promotions, so the number reflects real cost, not just media.

Then calculate it per channel too, because a blended CAC hides which sources are efficient and which are quietly draining budget.

## <span className="he">⚖️</span> CAC only means something next to value

<Callout type="key">

A CAC number in isolation is not good or bad. It's only meaningful compared to what a patient is worth.

Spending a few hundred dollars to acquire a one-time customer would be reckless. Spending the same to acquire a med spa patient who returns for years is often a steal.

</Callout>

That's why CAC and [lifetime value](/blog/medspa-patient-lifetime-value/) are two halves of one calculation, and looking at either alone leads to bad decisions, either starving profitable channels or over-feeding wasteful ones.

## <span className="he">⚡</span> The fastest way to lower it

The quickest CAC win isn't spending less. It's converting more.

If you double your [conversion rate](/medspa-cro/), you get twice the patients from the same spend, which cuts CAC in half without touching your budget.

That's the single highest-leverage move most practices have, and it sits on the landing page, not in the ad account.

## <span className="he">🌱</span> The levers that lower blended CAC over time

- **[Retargeting](/blog/medspa-retargeting/)** that recovers people you already paid to reach
- **[Referrals](/blog/medspa-referral-programs/),** the lowest-CAC channel there is
- **[Organic and local SEO](/medspa-seo/)** that book patients without a per-click cost
- **[Retention](/blog/medspa-client-retention/)** that spreads acquisition cost across more visits

Every one of these reduces how much you depend on paid clicks, and the less you rent traffic, the lower your blended CAC drifts.
