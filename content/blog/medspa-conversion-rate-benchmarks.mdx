- **Median:** 2 to 4 percent of visitors book a consult. **Good:** 6 percent plus. **Elite:** double digits.
- **Count the right thing:** booked consults divided by visitors, not form fills.
- **Judge by source:** paid search converts highest, paid social lowest.
- **The 3% to 11% story** came from dozens of tests, not one redesign.

</AtAGlance>

Short answer: for most med spas, **2 to 4 percent of website visitors turning into a booked consult is median, 6 percent or more is good, and double digits is elite.** But the number only means something once you define what you're counting.

## <span className="he">🎯</span> What counts as a conversion for a med spa

Three different things get called "conversion," and mixing them up is how owners fool themselves:

- **Form fill:** someone submitted a lead form
- **Booked consult:** that lead actually scheduled
- **Showed:** they walked in the door

This article uses *booked consult divided by website visitors*, because that's the number tied to revenue.

## <span className="he">📊</span> Benchmark table

| Traffic source | Poor | Median | Good | Elite |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Organic / local search | under 1.5% | 2 to 3% | 5 to 6% | 9%+ |
| Paid search | under 2% | 3 to 4% | 6 to 8% | 11%+ |
| Paid social | under 1% | 1.5 to 2.5% | 4 to 5% | 7%+ |
| Direct / referral | under 2% | 3 to 5% | 7 to 9% | 12%+ |

Paid search converts highest because intent is highest; paid social converts lowest because you interrupted someone. Judge your site against the right column for its source.

## <span className="he">⚠️</span> Why med spa sites underperform

Three recurring culprits: patient anxiety with no reassurance on the page, price opacity that stalls the decision, and form friction that bleeds the highest-intent visitors. Each is fixable without spending a dollar more on traffic.

## <span className="he">📈</span> The 3% to 11% breakdown

<Callout type="stat">

In six years running the testing program at LaserAway, founder Gabe Meierotto grew sitewide conversion from **3% to 11%**. It came from dozens of controlled tests, each adding a point or two, not one redesign.

</Callout>

Wins stacked because each test ran long enough to trust: [multi-step booking forms](/blog/medspa-multi-step-forms/), intro-offer framing, exit-intent offers, and treatment-page rewrites.

## <span className="he">🛠️</span> How to measure yours correctly

Track booked consults as a GA4 event, add call tracking for phone bookings, and segment by traffic source. A blended sitewide number hides which channel is actually broken.
