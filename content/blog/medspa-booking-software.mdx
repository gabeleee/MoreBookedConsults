- **There's no single "best."** The right pick depends on your size and clinical needs.
- **Rough 2026 pricing:** Vagaro from ~$30/mo, Mangomint ~$165, Boulevard ~$176 (med bundles higher), Zenoti quote-based.
- **The lens nobody uses:** how good is the *patient-facing* booking flow?
- **That flow is often your biggest conversion element,** so weigh it heavily.

</AtAGlance>

Most booking-software reviews rank platforms on back-office features: inventory, payroll, reporting.

Those matter, but they miss the thing that decides your bookings, which is how the software feels to the *patient* trying to schedule.

This comparison leads with that lens, then covers the features and pricing.

## <span className="he">📊</span> The 2026 comparison

Pricing below is current as of mid-2026 and changes often, so confirm before you commit.

| Platform | Starting price | Best for | Patient booking flow |
|---|---|---|---|
| Vagaro | ~$30/mo | Budget-conscious practices | Functional, less polished |
| Mangomint | ~$165/mo | Smaller injectable-led med spas | Excellent, top-rated UX |
| Boulevard | ~$176/mo (med bundles $369 to $421/location) | Client-experience-first practices | Premium, very polished |
| Zenoti | Quote (~$300 to $500+/location) | Enterprise and multi-location | Capable, enterprise-grade |

## <span className="he">🎯</span> The conversion lens nobody reviews with

Your online booking widget is embedded on your website, which means it's often the single most important conversion element you have.

If it's slow to load, buries availability, or forces an account before it shows a time, it leaks the very bookings your marketing worked to earn.

<Callout type="key">

Before you pick software for its reports, book a fake appointment on your own phone through each option's patient flow.

The one that feels effortless to *you* will feel effortless to patients, and that difference is worth more than most back-office features.

</Callout>

## <span className="he">🔎</span> The platforms, briefly

**Mangomint** earns some of the highest interface ratings in the category, and for a smaller med spa focused on injectables and light aesthetics, its excellent UX and clean patient flow are a strong fit.

**Boulevard** leads on client experience: the booking and intake flow is polished and fast, which suits practices that treat every patient touchpoint as brand.

**Zenoti** is the enterprise option, built for multi-location practices that need full clinical and business management, and priced accordingly.

**Vagaro** is the budget choice, bundling HIPAA-compliant records, intake, and scheduling at an accessible price, with a less premium feel.

## <span className="he">🔗</span> Integration with your lead forms

Software isn't an island; it has to fit your [funnel](/blog/medspa-lead-generation/).

The best setups let a [multi-step lead form](/blog/medspa-multi-step-forms/) hand off cleanly into booking, so a warm lead doesn't hit a second, clunkier system and drop off.

If your platform makes online booking painful, a purpose-built form on your site that feeds it can convert better than the native widget.

## <span className="he">🔁</span> Switching costs

Changing platforms is real work, so don't do it lightly.

Migrating client records, memberships, and gift cards takes time, and staff retraining costs a few weeks of friction, so switch only when the gain is clear.

Two features worth the switch for many med spas: a genuinely better patient booking flow, and native [membership management](/blog/medspa-membership-programs/) that drives recurring revenue.
