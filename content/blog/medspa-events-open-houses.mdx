- **An event's job is booked treatments,** not just a nice evening.
- **Invite your warm audience first:** existing patients and their friends.
- **Build in an event-only offer** with a deadline.
- **Book on the spot.** Excitement fades before people get home.

</AtAGlance>

Most med spa events are lovely and pointless: good wine, nice conversation, and almost no business the next morning.

The difference between an event that books and one that doesn't isn't the food or the decor. It's whether the whole thing is built to turn attendance into scheduled treatments.

## <span className="he">🎯</span> Decide what the event is for

An open house is a means, not an end, so name the end first.

For most practices the goal is retention and referrals: reward loyal patients, give them a reason to bring a friend, and book treatments while the room is warm.

<Callout type="key">

An event is a warm-audience play before it's a cold-acquisition play.

Your existing patients convert far better than strangers and bring the friends who become new patients, so build the guest list around them, not around filling a room with people who've never heard of you.

</Callout>

## <span className="he">🥂</span> Structure it to book

The event should march toward a booking, not away from it.

- **An event-only offer** with a real deadline, so there's a reason to act tonight.
- **Live demos or mini-treatments** that let people feel what you do.
- **A simple on-the-spot booking** with staff ready to schedule right there.
- **A referral hook** so guests who bring friends get rewarded.

Without these, an event is a party. With them, it's a booking engine.

## <span className="he">⏱️</span> Capture the booking before they leave

<Callout type="warning">

Excitement peaks in the room and fades on the drive home.

If you let guests leave with "I'll call to book," most won't. Schedule the appointment on-site, that night, while the enthusiasm is real.

</Callout>

Staff should be trained to book, not just mingle, and the offer should make booking now clearly better than booking later.

## <span className="he">🔁</span> Follow up fast

The people who didn't book on the night aren't lost, but they cool quickly.

Follow up within a day or two by [email](/blog/medspa-email-marketing/) or [text](/blog/medspa-sms-marketing/), reference the event, and extend the offer briefly, and you'll recover a chunk of the room that would otherwise drift away.
