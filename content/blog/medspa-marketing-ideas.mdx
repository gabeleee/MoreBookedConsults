<AtAGlance>

- **The best "idea" is boring:** convert the traffic you already have.
- **Acquisition plays:** referrals, intro offers, local partnerships, events.
- **Retention plays:** memberships, rebooking, email and SMS win-backs.
- **Skip deep discounting.** It trains bargain-hunters, not loyal patients.

</AtAGlance>

Search "med spa marketing ideas" and you'll get a hundred tactics with no priority order, which is how owners end up busy and broke.

The useful version sorts ideas by what actually books consults, and it starts with the least glamorous one.

## <span className="he">⚡</span> Start with the highest-return idea

Before any new campaign, fix the conversion rate on the traffic you already have.

It's not a "campaign," so it never makes these lists, but lifting your booking rate multiplies every other idea and costs no ad spend.

<Callout type="key">

A new promotion sends people to a leaky site, which wastes most of them.

Fixing [conversion](/medspa-cro/) first means every idea below works harder. Do this before you spend on anything new.

</Callout>

## <span className="he">📣</span> Acquisition ideas that work

Once your site converts, these bring the right new patients.

- **[Referral programs](/blog/medspa-referral-programs/).** Your cheapest, highest-trust channel.
- **[Intro offers](/blog/medspa-intro-offers/) framed around a second visit,** not a one-time discount.
- **Local partnerships.** Boutiques, gyms, and salons that share your patient.
- **[Events and open houses](/blog/medspa-events-open-houses/).** Convert your existing audience and generate referrals.

## <span className="he">🔁</span> Retention ideas that quietly out-earn them

The ideas owners overlook are the ones that keep patients coming back.

- **[Membership programs](/blog/medspa-membership-programs/)** that turn one visit into recurring revenue.
- **[Email](/blog/medspa-email-marketing/) and [SMS](/blog/medspa-sms-marketing/) win-back sequences** for lapsed patients.
- **A [rebooking habit](/blog/medspa-client-retention/)** so the next appointment is set before this one ends.

A repeat patient costs far less than a new one, so retention ideas usually beat acquisition ideas on return, even though they get less attention.

## <span className="he">🚫</span> Ideas to skip

- Deep, frequent discounting that trains patients to wait for a sale
- Buying followers or vanity metrics that never book
- Chasing every new channel instead of doing two well
