- **Marketing spend:** commonly 8–12% of revenue, up to ~15% in growth mode.

Plastic surgery is a high-revenue specialty, and knowing the benchmarks for both earnings and marketing spend helps you set a budget that grows the practice rather than guessing.

Here are realistic 2026 figures, and what they mean for how much to invest in getting patients.

## <span className="he">💵</span> Revenue and income benchmarks

Plastic surgeon earnings vary widely by practice type and maturity.

As of 2026, compensation commonly runs from a **median around $750,000** into seven figures, with cosmetic-focused surgeons often averaging **above $1 million** and top earners well beyond that.

Cash-pay cosmetic practices tend to out-earn reconstructive-heavy ones, and practice-owner economics can be substantially higher than employed-surgeon pay once ownership profit is included.

## <span className="he">📊</span> Marketing spend benchmarks

<Callout type="key">

Established plastic surgery practices commonly allocate roughly **8 to 12 percent** of gross revenue to marketing, with growth-mode practices pushing toward **12 to 15 percent**, and healthy spend generally staying under 15 percent.

On a practice doing $1.5M, that's about $120,000 to $180,000 a year, or roughly $10,000 to $15,000 a month.

</Callout>

The right number depends on your growth goals: a mature practice defending its position spends less than one aggressively pursuing growth.

## <span className="he">⚖️</span> Why the spend pays off (when it converts)

High patient value is what makes this spend rational.

With a booked surgery worth thousands and patient lifetime value often in the five figures over a few years, a well-run program returns far more than it costs, and a favorable blended [acquisition cost](/blog/plastic-surgery-patient-acquisition-cost/) against that value is very profitable.

<Callout type="warning">

The key word is well-run. Spending 12 percent of revenue on a leaky funnel wastes most of it, so conversion has to come first.

</Callout>

## <span className="he">🎯</span> What the numbers mean for you

Set your [budget](/blog/medspa-marketing-budget/) as a deliberate percentage of revenue tied to your growth goals, not a random figure, and make sure your [funnel converts](/plastic-surgery-cro/) before you scale it.

<Callout type="note">

Figures reflect widely cited 2026 benchmarks and vary by source, practice type, and market. Treat them as directional, not precise.

</Callout>
