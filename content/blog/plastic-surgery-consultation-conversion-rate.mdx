Short answer: a healthy surgical practice converts roughly **2–4% of website visitors into an inquiry, 40–60% of inquiries into a booked consultation, and 30–50% of consultations into surgery.** Multiply those and the average site turns a low single-digit fraction of visitors into cases, which is exactly why small improvements at the middle of the funnel are worth so much.

## The four-stage funnel

A plastic surgery practice doesn't have one conversion rate; it has a chain of them. Each stage is a separate benchmark and a separate fix.

| Stage | Poor | Median | Good |
|---|---|---|---|
| Visitor → inquiry | under 1.5% | 2–4% | 6%+ |
| Inquiry → booked consult | under 30% | 40–60% | 70%+ |
| Consult → surgery | under 25% | 30–50% | 60%+ |
| Visitor → surgery (blended) | under 0.3% | 0.5–1% | 1.5%+ |

Judge each stage on its own row. A practice with great surgeons and a 30%-inquiry-to-consult rate doesn't have a talent problem, it has a follow-up problem.

## Why website conversion is the cheapest stage to fix

The visitor-to-inquiry stage is the only one you can improve **without spending a dollar more on ads and without adding staff.** It's just the site: [inquiry forms](/blog/plastic-surgery-website-design/), procedure pages, [before/after galleries](/blog/plastic-surgery-before-after-galleries/), and [financing clarity](/blog/plastic-surgery-financing-marketing/). That's why our [plastic surgery CRO](/plastic-surgery-cro/) work starts here, the leverage is highest and the cost is lowest.

## What moves each stage

- **Visitor → inquiry:** shorter forms, clearer procedure pricing signals, ungated galleries.
- **Inquiry → consult:** [speed-to-lead and follow-up sequences](/blog/plastic-surgery-lead-follow-up/), and a [patient coordinator](/blog/patient-coordinator-consult-conversion/) who owns the booking.
- **Consult → surgery:** [financing presented well](/blog/plastic-surgery-financing-marketing/), and [virtual consult options](/blog/virtual-plastic-surgery-consultations/) for out-of-market patients.

## How to measure without fooling yourself

Surgical decision cycles run 3–12 months, so last-click attribution lies. Track cohorts by inquiry month, not by close month, and give each cohort a full window to mature before you judge it. Call tracking matters more here than for a med spa, because a large share of high-intent surgical inquiries come by phone.

## Med spa vs. plastic surgery conversion

Med spa funnels are shorter and higher-volume; a med spa can support more frequent testing, and its consult is often the treatment itself. Surgical funnels are longer, higher-value, and gated by financing and galleries. The [med spa conversion benchmarks](/blog/medspa-conversion-rate-benchmarks/) are the sister piece to this one, same discipline, different economics. Both are aesthetic practice marketing; the levers just sit at different stages.

## FAQ

**What's a good plastic surgery consultation conversion rate?** 40–60% of inquiries booking a consult is median; 70%+ is strong. Below 30% almost always points to slow follow-up.

**Should I focus on more traffic or better conversion?** At surgical case values, one extra converted consult a month can exceed a marketing retainer, fix conversion first.
