A single long form asks a stranger for everything at once. A multi-step form asks one easy question, then builds momentum. For med spas, the multi-step version consistently wins, and it's usually the single highest-impact test on the site.

## <span className="he">🎯</span> Why single long forms bleed leads

Commitment psychology: the first click is the hardest. Once someone answers a low-friction question, sunk-cost momentum carries them through the rest. A wall of fields shown up front triggers the opposite, a quick bounce before anyone commits.

## <span className="he">🧩</span> The LaserAway form anatomy

The booking form our founder tested at LaserAway broke the ask into steps that escalated gently:

1. **Treatment interest:** a low-friction, high-engagement opener (buttons, not a dropdown)
2. **A qualifying question:** history or an age band
3. **Contact details:** asked last, once the visitor is already invested

What got tested along the way: step count, icons versus text on the option cards, question ordering, auto-advance on selection, and the progress indicator. Each variable was its own controlled test.

## <span className="he">⚠️</span> Fields that quietly kill conversion

- Phone number asked before the visitor is invested
- Free-text boxes where a tap would do
- Required fields that aren't actually required to follow up
- A "message" field that makes people feel they must compose something

## <span className="he">📊</span> Measuring per-step drop-off

Instrument each step as its own event so you can see exactly where people quit. The step with the biggest drop is your next test, not a guess.
