<AtAGlance>

- **Start marketing before you open.** A waitlist beats a cold day one.
- **Highest-leverage move:** a founding-member waitlist tied to an offer.
- **Stage your channels:** fast levers (ads, referrals) carry slow ones (SEO).
- **This cluster is marketing-only.** Licensing and buildout live elsewhere.

</AtAGlance>

A new med spa's biggest marketing risk isn't a bad ad. It's opening to silence. The practices that launch well start building demand months before the doors open, so day one has a calendar instead of a countdown.

## <span className="he">🗓️</span> Before you open: the waitlist

<Callout type="key">

The single highest-leverage launch move is a pre-opening waitlist tied to a founding-member offer. It captures demand early, funds your first week, and turns opening day into momentum. See [pre-opening marketing and waitlists](/blog/medspa-pre-opening-marketing/).

</Callout>

While the waitlist builds, get the fundamentals live: a [Google Business Profile](/blog/medspa-google-business-profile/) (even "opening soon"), a simple site with a [working booking flow](/medspa-cro/), and your social presence seeded with the [brand](/blog/medspa-branding-naming/).

## <span className="he">🎉</span> Opening week

Your [grand opening](/blog/medspa-grand-opening/) should convert the waitlist into booked treatments and generate the first wave of reviews and referrals, the fuel local ranking runs on. Front-load it with an event, a founding-member push, and a review ask built into every visit.

## <span className="he">🛠️</span> The first six months

Sequence matters when everything is new:

- **Months 1 to 2:** paid ads and referrals for immediate bookings, [reviews](/blog/medspa-get-more-reviews/) from every visit
- **Months 2 to 4:** [local SEO](/blog/how-to-rank-medspa-google-maps/) foundations that compound
- **Months 3 to 6:** [retention systems](/blog/medspa-membership-programs/) so first-timers come back

The full month-by-month version is the [six-month marketing plan](/blog/medspa-marketing-plan/).
