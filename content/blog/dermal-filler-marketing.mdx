Dermal fillers carry more emotion than almost any med spa treatment, because patients have all seen filler go wrong.

That fear is the central fact of filler marketing, and the practices that address it directly are the ones that book.

## <span className="he">📈</span> Demand and seasonality

Filler demand is steady year-round, with the same reliability as other injectables and mild bumps before big events.

It skews toward patients who've often already tried Botox and are ready for more, so it tends to sell well to an existing, trusting patient base.

That makes fillers a natural [retention](/blog/medspa-client-retention/) and upsell play, not just an acquisition target.

## <span className="he">💰</span> Offer economics

<Callout type="key">

Fillers cost more per syringe than neurotoxins but generate higher revenue per visit, so a retained filler patient is especially valuable.

Price per syringe or area if you like, but market the result and the artistry, because patients aren't buying cc's, they're buying a face they recognize.

</Callout>

Structure around a plan, since most filler results are maintained over time, and that ongoing relationship drives real [lifetime value](/blog/medspa-patient-lifetime-value/).

## <span className="he">⚖️</span> Ad-policy constraints

<Callout type="warning">

Fillers face the same injectable ad restrictions as Botox: limits on branded names, health claims, and before-and-after imagery.

Run compliant ads, but expect to build trust through your portfolio, [reviews](/blog/medspa-get-more-reviews/), and referrals as much as through paid creative.

</Callout>

## <span className="he">🎯</span> The conversion angle

The filler patient's fear is looking overfilled, unnatural, or "done."

Sell the injector's eye and restraint: natural, subtle, tasteful results, backed by a real [portfolio](/blog/medspa-before-after-photos/) shown with consent, and let your [page](/medspa-cro/) prove you're the safe pair of hands, because in fillers, trust in the artist is the entire decision.
