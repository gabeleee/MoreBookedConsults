- **Every treatment needs its own page and angle.** One generic page can't serve all.

- **Match the searcher's real intent,** not a template.
- **Never guarantee results,** for compliance and credibility both.

A med spa sells many treatments, and each one is really a different marketing problem wearing the same lab coat.

The practices that win search and convert well don't lump everything onto one services page. They give each treatment its own page, tuned to how that treatment actually sells.

## <span className="he">🎯</span> Why one page can't do it all

Someone searching "laser hair removal cost" and someone searching "Botox near me" are different people with different questions, seasons, and worries.

A single generic page can't rank for both or answer either well, so it loses to competitors who built a page for each.

Dedicated treatment pages match intent, and matching intent is most of the battle in both [SEO](/medspa-seo/) and [conversion](/medspa-cro/).

## <span className="he">🔀</span> The four things that change per treatment

<Callout type="key">

When you move from one treatment to another, four factors shift, and your page has to shift with them:

Demand seasonality, offer economics, ad-policy constraints, and the conversion angle.

</Callout>

- **Seasonality:** body treatments spike before summer; injectables run steadier
- **Offer economics:** some treatments sell as single visits, others as [packages or memberships](/blog/medspa-membership-programs/)
- **Ad policy:** [restrictions](/blog/medspa-healthcare-ad-policy/) are tighter for some categories, especially weight loss
- **Conversion angle:** the fear or desire that actually moves that patient

Get these right per page and each treatment sells on its own terms.

Each guide below applies that same framework to a specific treatment, with its own seasonality, economics, and ad-policy notes:

- **Injectables:** [Botox](/blog/botox-marketing/), [dermal fillers](/blog/dermal-filler-marketing/), and [lip filler](/blog/lip-filler-marketing/)

- **Skin & resurfacing:** [Morpheus8](/blog/morpheus8-marketing/), [HydraFacial](/blog/hydrafacial-marketing/), [chemical peels](/blog/chemical-peel-marketing/), [IPL / photofacial](/blog/ipl-photofacial-marketing/), and [laser resurfacing](/blog/laser-resurfacing-marketing/)
- **Hair & removal:** [laser hair removal](/blog/laser-hair-removal-marketing/), [tattoo removal](/blog/tattoo-removal-marketing/), and [PRP / hair restoration](/blog/prp-marketing/)

## <span className="he">⚖️</span> One rule across all of them

<Callout type="warning">

Whatever the treatment, never promise or guarantee results.

Lead with honest expectations, real trust signals, and a clear [offer](/blog/medspa-intro-offers/), because guarantees are both a compliance risk and a fast way to lose credibility with a smart patient.

</Callout>
