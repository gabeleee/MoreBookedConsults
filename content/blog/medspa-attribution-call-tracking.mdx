- **Perfect attribution doesn't exist;** good-enough attribution does.
- **Combine three things:** ask patients, track calls, track form conversions.
- **Call tracking is essential** if phone bookings matter to you.
- **Do it HIPAA-aware:** track the source, not the private conversation.

"Half my marketing works, I just don't know which half" is the oldest problem in the business, and most med spas still live it.

You'll never get to perfect attribution, but you can get close enough to stop wasting money on channels that don't book patients.

## <span className="he">🧩</span> Why attribution is hard

Patients rarely travel a clean, single path.

Someone might see a TikTok, later search your name, read a review, and finally call, so which channel gets the credit is genuinely fuzzy.

That's why chasing perfect, last-click certainty is a trap. The goal is directional truth: which channels reliably show up in the journeys that end in a booking.

## <span className="he">🛠️</span> The simple stack that works

<Callout type="key">

Three cheap methods together get you most of the answer:

Ask every new patient how they found you, use call tracking to attribute phone bookings, and track online conversions from your forms.

</Callout>

None is perfect alone, but combined they reveal which channels drive booked, kept patients and which just generate activity, and that's enough to reallocate [budget](/blog/medspa-marketing-budget/) intelligently.

Make "how did you hear about us" a standard intake question, because that one habit fills the gaps automation misses.

## <span className="he">📞</span> Call tracking, done right

If a real share of your bookings arrive by phone, they're invisible without call tracking.

Assigning a trackable number to each channel lets you see that, say, your Google Ads drive calls even when they drive few form fills, which stops you from cutting a channel that's actually working.

<Callout type="warning">

Calls can involve health information, so use a provider that supports healthcare compliance, secure or limit recordings, and never pass sensitive content to ad platforms.

Track the source and the outcome, not the private details of the conversation.

</Callout>

## <span className="he">🎯</span> Turn measurement into decisions

Attribution only pays off if it changes what you do.

Use it to make [cost per booked consult](/blog/medspa-kpis/) real per channel, then move money toward what books patients and away from what doesn't, and skim the [marketing-laws node](/blog/medspa-marketing-laws/) to keep your tracking compliant.
