Before-and-after photos are among the most persuasive assets a med spa has, and among the easiest to get legally wrong.

A patient's images are protected health information, so using them casually isn't a gray area, it's a real violation waiting to happen.

## <span className="he">✍️</span> Consent comes first, every time

<Callout type="warning">

Never post a patient's before-and-after without specific, written consent for that exact use.

A general treatment form is not marketing consent. Get a signed release that names where and how the images may be used, whether social, ads, your site, and printed materials, and stay inside what it grants.

</Callout>

Keep the signed releases on file, let patients decline without any pressure, and honor a later request to remove images, because consent isn't a one-time formality, it's an ongoing obligation.

## <span className="he">📱</span> Where the rules change: organic vs paid

What you can post on your own channels and what you can run as an ad are two different things.

On your own [Instagram](/blog/medspa-instagram-marketing/), site, or profile, before-and-afters with proper consent are fine and effective.

In paid ads, especially on Meta, before-and-after imagery is heavily restricted, so plan for your ad creative to rely on other assets even when your organic content leans on results. The [healthcare ad policy node](/blog/medspa-healthcare-ad-policy/) covers the specifics.

## <span className="he">📸</span> Shooting photos that build trust

Persuasion here comes from credibility, and credibility comes from consistency.

- **Same setup for both shots:** lighting, angle, distance, background
- **No misleading editing** that overstates the result
- **Realistic outcomes,** not cherry-picked extremes presented as typical
- **Clear, honest framing** of what the treatment actually did

<Callout type="key">

A consistent, honest pair builds more trust than a dramatic but obviously staged one.

Prospective patients can tell when a photo is manipulated, and the moment they doubt one image, they doubt all of them.

</Callout>

Used right, with consent and honesty, results photos become one of the strongest [conversion assets](/medspa-cro/) on your site.
