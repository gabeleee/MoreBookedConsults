Search "best med spa lead generation companies" and you will get a dozen ranked lists, most written by lead-gen companies ranking themselves.

The honest answer is that there is no single best, because the models behind these companies are so different that comparing them by name misses the point.

What actually matters is which model fits your risk tolerance, and then whether the specific provider runs that model well.

This guide sorts the field into four models, says who each suits, and gives you the four questions that judge any company faster than a leaderboard.

## The four models, and who each suits

Almost every med spa lead-generation company runs one of four models.

- **Shared-lead brokers.** They sell the same prospect to three or four spas at once. Cheapest per lead on the sticker, worst converting in reality, because you are racing competitors to the same phone. Suits almost no one who understands the tradeoff.
- **Full-service ad agencies.** They run your ads on a retainer plus ad spend, usually to your website or a landing page. Suits a practice that wants a full marketing partner and is comfortable carrying the ad-spend risk itself.
- **DIY ad platforms and tools.** Software that helps you run your own lead ads. Cheapest if your time is free and you know what you are doing, risky as your whole strategy if you don't.
- **Pay-per-lead operators.** They build and run the funnel, carry the ad spend, and sell you exclusive leads at a flat price per lead. Suits a practice that wants leads without managing ads or carrying the risk.

The right choice is the one that matches how involved you want to be and how much risk you want to hold, not the one at the top of someone's list.

## Why the model matters more than the name

Two companies running the same model will give you broadly similar outcomes, and two companies running different models will give you wildly different ones.

That is why a ranked list of names is close to useless.

A shared-lead broker with a slick website is still a shared-lead broker.

<Callout type="key">

The single biggest predictor of whether bought leads work is exclusive versus shared, and that is a property of the model, not the brand.

Sort by model first. Only then does comparing individual providers make sense.

</Callout>

Most of the reputation problem the category has comes from one model, shared leads, being sold aggressively under many different names. The [exclusive vs shared leads](/exclusive-medspa-leads/) page explains exactly why that model disappoints and why it keeps getting resold anyway.

## The four questions that judge any company

Skip the ranked lists and ask any provider these four questions.

**1. Are the leads exclusive to me?** If they are shared, your close rate is capped before you start.

**2. How fast are they delivered?** Real-time with instant follow-up, or a batch tomorrow morning? A [slow lead is a cold lead](/blog/speed-to-lead-medspa/).

**3. What counts as a billable lead, and what gets credited?** A real provider defines this precisely and credits duplicates and out-of-area submissions.

**4. Who carries the ad spend if the funnel underperforms?** If the answer is you, you are holding the risk. If the answer is them, they are.

A provider that answers all four cleanly is worth more than one at the top of a list that dodges them.
