In elective plastic surgery, the most common reason a consult doesn't become a surgery isn't doubt about the surgeon. It's cost.

Financing is how you dissolve that objection, and treating it as a conversion tool rather than an afterthought books surgeries you'd otherwise lose.

## <span className="he">💸</span> Cost is the objection financing solves

<Callout type="key">

Most stalled surgical decisions stall on price. Financing reframes a large one-time cost as a manageable monthly payment, which turns "not right now" into "I can do this."

</Callout>

That reframing is powerful precisely because the interest and trust are usually already there, so removing the money barrier is often all that's left between consult and booking.

## <span className="he">🏦</span> The main vendors (as of mid-2026)

Several providers dominate elective-surgery financing, and they differ in ways that matter.

- **CareCredit:** long-established, with promotional no-interest-if-paid-in-full periods
- **PatientFi:** installment plans with a soft credit check to apply
- **Cherry:** buy-now-pay-later with high approval rates and true 0% APR offers, higher credit limits
- **Alphaeon Credit:** elective-care focused, with deferred-interest promotional options

<Callout type="note">

Many practices offer more than one, often pairing a traditional option with a buy-now-pay-later provider to approve more patients across credit profiles. Vendor terms change, so confirm current specifics directly.

</Callout>

## <span className="he">📄</span> Where to place it on-site

Financing should appear wherever cost anxiety lives, not hidden on a page nobody finds.

Put it on procedure pages, near the [consult request](/blog/plastic-surgery-website-design/), and on a clear dedicated financing page, so a cost-worried prospect sees a path forward exactly when the worry hits.

Surfacing it early does quiet conversion work before the consult even happens.

## <span className="he">🤝</span> Let the coordinator close with it

The highest-value moment for financing is the consult itself.

A confident [patient coordinator](/blog/patient-coordinator-consult-conversion/) who presents financing naturally, as the normal way patients proceed, converts far more than a brochure left on a table, because they can match the option to the patient in real time.

That combination, visible on-site and presented well in person, is what makes financing a genuine [conversion](/plastic-surgery-cro/) lever.
