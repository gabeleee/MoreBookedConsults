Tattoo removal is one of the higher-intent treatments a med spa can market, because almost nobody searches for it casually.

That intent is an asset, but it comes with a responsibility: to set honest expectations about a long, patient process.

## <span className="he">📈</span> Demand and seasonality

Tattoo removal demand is fairly steady year-round, without the sharp seasonal swings of body or skin treatments.

What defines it instead is intent: people searching for tattoo removal generally want the tattoo gone, which makes them ready-to-book prospects rather than browsers.

That high-intent, low-seasonality profile makes it a dependable [paid-search](/blog/medspa-google-ads/) treatment.

## <span className="he">💰</span> Offer economics: a long series

<Callout type="key">

Full removal typically takes many sessions over many months, so tattoo removal is inherently a long series, and pricing should reflect that.

Package or per-session-with-a-plan structures fit the reality and add up to significant revenue per committed patient.

</Callout>

The honest [intro-to-plan](/blog/medspa-intro-offers/) framing works well: a first session that begins a clearly explained multi-visit journey.

## <span className="he">⚖️</span> Ad-policy constraints

Tattoo removal is a laser device treatment without injectable or prescription-drug restrictions, so paid and social have room, and high intent makes search especially effective.

Keep claims about complete removal and timelines honest, since results vary by ink and skin, but the ad-policy friction is low.

## <span className="he">🎯</span> The conversion angle

<Callout type="warning">

The biggest risk is overselling speed. Patients who expect two sessions and need ten leave unhappy and review accordingly.

</Callout>

Convert on honesty and expertise: realistic timelines, clear factors that affect results, and confidence in your technology, and let your [page](/medspa-cro/) treat the patient as someone making a committed, multi-month decision, because trustworthy realism books the serious buyer.
