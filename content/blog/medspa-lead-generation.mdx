<AtAGlance>

- **A lead is not a booking.** Most practices leak between the two.
- **The funnel is: attract, capture, follow up, book, show.**
- **Speed of follow-up wins.** Respond in minutes, not hours.
- **Converting leads beats generating more of them,** almost every time.

</AtAGlance>

"Lead generation" makes it sound like the goal is leads, but a lead you never convert is just an expense.

The goal is booked, kept appointments, and most med spas lose more revenue in the gap between lead and booking than they'd gain from any new source of leads.

## <span className="he">🧭</span> The funnel, honestly

A working funnel has stages, and a break in any one wastes everything upstream.

- **Attract.** [Local SEO](/medspa-seo/), referrals, and ads bring the right people.
- **Capture.** A fast, simple way to raise a hand: a form, a call, a booking widget.
- **Follow up.** Reach the lead fast, before interest cools.
- **Book.** Turn interest into a scheduled appointment.
- **Show.** Reminders that get them through the door.

Owners obsess over "attract" and ignore the rest, which is exactly backwards.

## <span className="he">⚡</span> The leak is usually follow-up

<Callout type="key">

If you get leads but few bookings, the problem is almost never lead volume. It's what happens after the lead comes in.

Slow follow-up, a clunky booking step, or no nurture at all lets paid-for leads go cold. Fix that, and your existing leads suddenly convert.

</Callout>

Lead interest decays by the minute, so responding in minutes rather than hours often decides who gets the booking, and it's one of the cheapest fixes there is.

## <span className="he">🔁</span> Convert before you scale

Spending more to generate leads on a leaky funnel just wastes more money faster.

Fix [conversion](/medspa-cro/) and follow-up first, so every lead is worth more, and then scale the sources, because scaling a funnel that converts is growth while scaling one that doesn't is just a bigger bill.

## <span className="he">🌱</span> Feed it with owned channels

Paid leads work, but they stop the moment you stop paying.

Build [local SEO](/medspa-seo/) and [referrals](/blog/medspa-referral-programs/) alongside them, so a growing share of your calendar fills from channels you own rather than rent, and your cost per booking falls over time instead of rising.
