<AtAGlance>

- **Retargeting is often your highest-ROI ad spend:** warm audience, low cost.
- **Stay HIPAA-aware:** retarget on general engagement, not treatment pages.
- **Cap frequency** so warm doesn't become annoying.
- **It lowers overall acquisition cost** by recovering people who almost booked.

</AtAGlance>

Most people who visit a med spa site don't book on the first visit, and without retargeting, nearly all of them are gone for good.

Retargeting brings them back, and because they already know you, it's usually the most efficient ad money a practice spends.

## <span className="he">💰</span> Why it works so well

A cold ad has to earn trust from scratch. A retargeting ad reaches someone who already visited your site or engaged with your content, so the trust is partly built.

That warmer audience converts at higher rates and lower cost, which is why retargeting so often beats cold prospecting on return and quietly lowers your overall [acquisition cost](/blog/medspa-customer-acquisition-cost/).

<Callout type="key">

You already paid to get that visitor once. Retargeting is how you stop wasting the 95 percent who left without booking.

</Callout>

## <span className="he">⚖️</span> Doing it HIPAA-aware

<Callout type="warning">

Retargeting people based on the specific treatment or condition they viewed can cross into protected health information, which is exactly what you must not leak to ad platforms.

Retarget on general engagement, like visiting your site, rather than on treatment-specific pages, and use setups that don't pass sensitive data to the platform.

</Callout>

The safe pattern is broad and general: bring back site visitors as a group, not "people who looked at the Botox page," and lead with helpful messaging rather than anything that reveals what they were considering.

Read the [healthcare ad policy node](/blog/medspa-healthcare-ad-policy/) for the boundaries.

## <span className="he">🎯</span> Campaigns worth running

- **Site visitors** who didn't book, brought back with results and a clear offer.
- **Engaged social audiences** who watched or interacted but didn't act.
- **Past patients** re-engaged with new treatments or seasonal offers.

Keep frequency capped so a helpful reminder never turns into an annoyance, and point every click at a [page built to convert](/medspa-cro/), because the whole value of retargeting evaporates if the landing page leaks.
