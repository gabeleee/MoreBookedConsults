Short answer: to rank a med spa in the Google Maps local pack, you optimize three things Google weighs, **relevance, distance, and prominence**, through a dialed-in Google Business Profile, steady reviews, consistent citations, and treatment pages that match what your city searches. There's no single switch; it's a stack, and it compounds.

## The three ranking factors

Google ranks the local pack on:

- **Relevance**, how well your profile and site match the search ("botox near me," "medspa [city]").
- **Distance**, how close you are to the searcher. You can't move your building, but relevance and prominence can outweigh a closer, weaker competitor.
- **Prominence**, how well-known and trusted you are: review count and quality, citations, and your website's authority.

Everything below moves one of those three.

## Google Business Profile is the foundation

Your [Google Business Profile](/blog/medspa-google-business-profile/) is the single highest-leverage asset in local search, correct primary category, complete services, real photos, accurate hours, and regular posts. Most med spas leave half of it blank. Filling it out well is the cheapest ranking gain available.

## Reviews move the needle hardest

Review count, velocity, and recency are among the strongest prominence signals, and they're also what convinces the human reading the pack. A steady [review-generation habit](/blog/medspa-get-more-reviews/) beats a one-time push. Respond to every review, and keep replies HIPAA-safe (never confirm someone is a patient).

## Citations and consistency

[Citations](/blog/medspa-citations-directories/), your name, address, and phone listed consistently across directories, reinforce that you're a real, established business. The priority isn't volume; it's consistency. One wrong old address on a directory can quietly split your signal.

## On-page: treatment-by-city relevance

The map pack and the organic results below it feed each other. Pages that clearly cover the treatments you offer, in the market you serve, raise relevance for both. Start with [keyword research](/blog/medspa-keyword-research/) to find what your city actually searches, and work through a [technical SEO checklist](/blog/medspa-seo-checklist/) so nothing blocks indexing. Multiple locations? That's its own discipline, see [multi-location local SEO](/blog/medspa-multi-location-seo/).

## How to measure

Track your map-pack position for your core treatment + city queries (from a consistent location), your Google Business Profile calls and direction requests, and organic bookings by landing page. Rankings without booking growth mean the site is leaking, a local SEO problem and a [conversion problem](/medspa-cro/) are different leaks.

## FAQ

**How long does local SEO take for a med spa?** Profile and review gains can show in weeks; competitive treatment + city rankings usually take a few months of consistent work.
