Laser hair removal is one of the most searchable, most seasonal treatments a med spa offers, and one of the most commonly mis-sold.

The practices that win it understand two things: it's a package, and the best time to sell it is before patients think to buy.

## <span className="he">📈</span> Demand and seasonality

<Callout type="key">

Search demand spikes in spring as summer approaches, but the smart play is to market the fall and winter start.

The treatment needs a series over several months and works best away from sun exposure, so an autumn start means smooth results by summer, and it captures patients before the crowded spring rush.

</Callout>

Marketing against the obvious season is a real edge here, because you reach ready buyers when competitors aren't shouting.

## <span className="he">💰</span> Offer economics: sell the series

Laser hair removal doesn't work in one session, so selling it as one is both misleading and leaves money behind.

Package or membership pricing fits the clinical reality, raises revenue per patient, and sets honest expectations, which is exactly the kind of [intro offer that acquires a real patient](/blog/medspa-intro-offers/) rather than a one-time bargain-hunter.

## <span className="he">⚖️</span> Ad-policy constraints

This is a device treatment, so it avoids the prescription-drug and injectable restrictions that hamstring Botox and weight-loss ads.

That gives [Google Ads](/blog/medspa-google-ads/) and paid social more room to work, which is why laser hair removal is often a great paid-search treatment. Keep claims honest, but the ad friction is genuinely lower.

## <span className="he">🎯</span> The conversion angle

Patients want to be done with shaving and waxing, so sell the finished result, not the machine.

Frame it as a season-long investment that ends in freedom from routine, set clear expectations about the series, and let your [page](/medspa-cro/) make starting now feel like the obvious step toward being ready by summer.
