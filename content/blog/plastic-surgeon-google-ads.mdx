Google Ads put a plastic surgery practice in front of prospects at the exact moment they're searching for a procedure, which is powerful, and expensive.

The high stakes cut both ways: the traffic is valuable, but so is every wasted click, so paid search rewards discipline over enthusiasm.

## <span className="he">🎯</span> Why paid search fits surgery

Someone searching "rhinoplasty near me" or "tummy tuck surgeon" has real intent, and paid search puts you in front of them immediately.

For a high-value procedure, even an expensive click can be worth it, because a single booked surgery can cover a lot of ad spend.

The mechanics are the same as any [paid search](/blog/medspa-google-ads/), but the stakes per conversion are far higher.

## <span className="he">💰</span> Manage the right number

<Callout type="key">

Cost-per-click for competitive procedure terms is high, because the value per patient is large and incumbents bid hard. So the number to manage is cost per booked consult, not cost per click.

A pricey click that books a high-value surgery is a bargain; a cheap click that never converts is pure waste.

</Callout>

Track cost per consult and feed it into your [acquisition-cost](/blog/plastic-surgery-patient-acquisition-cost/) math, and judge the channel on booked surgeries, not traffic.

## <span className="he">⚡</span> Conversion is the biggest lever

<Callout type="warning">

The most common mistake is paying premium prices for high-intent clicks and then leaking them with a weak funnel.

Poor galleries, hidden financing, and slow follow-up waste expensive traffic, so [fix conversion](/plastic-surgery-cro/) before you scale ads.

</Callout>

Because clicks cost so much here, improving conversion rate moves cost per consult more than any bidding tweak, which is why the funnel comes first.

## <span className="he">⚖️</span> Compliance and when to run it

Health advertising restrictions apply, so keep claims honest and creative compliant.

And weigh paid search against [SEO](/blog/medspa-seo-vs-google-ads/): ads give speed but rented traffic, while SEO compounds, so most practices run ads for immediate consults while building organic underneath, and only scale ad spend once the funnel reliably converts.
