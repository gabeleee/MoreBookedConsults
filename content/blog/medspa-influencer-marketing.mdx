- **Local beats large.** A creator whose followers can visit you is worth more.
- **Micro-influencers** with engaged local audiences drive real bookings.
- **FTC disclosure is mandatory** and clear, not buried in hashtags.
- **You're responsible** for making sure partners disclose properly.

</AtAGlance>

Influencer marketing for a med spa isn't about follower counts, and chasing big numbers is exactly how practices waste money here.

A med spa is a local business, so the only reach that matters is reach that can actually walk through your door.

## <span className="he">📍</span> Local and small beats famous and big

A creator with a million distant followers can't fill your calendar, because almost none of them live near you.

A local micro-influencer with a few thousand engaged followers in your city is far more valuable, because their audience is your audience, and their recommendation carries real trust.

<Callout type="key">

Reach that can't book you is vanity. Local reach is revenue.

Pick partners by where their followers live and how much those followers trust them, not by total follower count.

</Callout>

## <span className="he">🤝</span> Structuring a partnership that works

The best partnerships feel genuine, not bought.

Work with creators who'd actually like your practice, give them a real experience worth talking about, and let them share it in their own voice rather than scripting a stiff ad.

Set clear expectations on deliverables and disclosure up front, and treat it as a relationship rather than a one-off transaction, because authentic ongoing advocacy beats a single paid post.

## <span className="he">⚖️</span> The FTC rules you can't skip

<Callout type="warning">

Any material connection, free treatment, payment, product, or a discount, must be clearly and conspicuously disclosed in the post.

A disclosure buried in a wall of hashtags doesn't satisfy the rule. It has to be obvious, and as the practice, you're on the hook for making sure partners comply.

</Callout>

Build disclosure into your agreement, confirm it's done correctly on every post, and skim the [marketing-laws node](/blog/medspa-marketing-laws/) before you start, because an undisclosed endorsement is a compliance problem for you, not just the creator.
