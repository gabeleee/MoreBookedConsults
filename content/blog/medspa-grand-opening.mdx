- **Success is booked treatments,** not turnout.
- **Convert your waitlist first;** they're your warmest guests.
- **Build the event to book:** event-day offer, demos, on-site scheduling.
- **Follow up fast** before the buzz fades.

</AtAGlance>

A grand opening feels like a celebration, but for a new med spa it's a conversion event, and treating it like a party is how launches fizzle.

The goal isn't a full room. It's a full calendar the week after.

## <span className="he">🎯</span> Convert the waitlist first

If you did the [pre-opening work](/blog/medspa-pre-opening-marketing/), you arrive with a waitlist, and those people are the whole point.

Invite them first, honor the founding-member offer you promised, and make the opening the moment they finally book, because a warm local audience converts and refers far better than an anonymous crowd.

Their friends are your next-best guests, so make it easy for founders to bring one.

## <span className="he">🥂</span> Build the event to book

<Callout type="key">

The same rule as any [event that books](/blog/medspa-events-open-houses/) applies here: structure the whole thing toward a scheduled appointment.

An event-day offer with a real deadline, live demos or mini-treatments, and staff ready to book on the spot turn attendance into appointments before anyone leaves.

</Callout>

Excitement peaks in the room and fades on the drive home, so capture the booking that night, not "call us later."

## <span className="he">📇</span> Capture everyone else

Not every guest books immediately, but none should leave untracked.

Collect contact info from everyone, whether through the event offer, a sign-in, or a giveaway, so the people who came but didn't commit become a follow-up list rather than a lost opportunity.

## <span className="he">🔁</span> Follow up before the buzz fades

The days right after are when a launch is won or lost.

Follow up fast with everyone who attended, reference the event, extend the offer briefly, and convert the stragglers, then focus on turning your new patients into [regulars](/blog/medspa-client-retention/) with a rebooking habit from visit one.

A grand opening that books is just the first chapter of the [new-practice plan](/blog/how-to-market-new-medspa/), not the finish line.
