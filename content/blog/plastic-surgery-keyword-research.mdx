Keyword research decides what your plastic surgery SEO actually goes after, and the most common mistake is chasing big numbers instead of real intent.

The goal isn't traffic, it's consults, so the right keywords are the ones searched by people close to booking, not the ones with the biggest volume.

## <span className="he">🎯</span> Intent tiers that matter

<Callout type="key">

The keywords worth targeting are high-intent: procedure-plus-location searches and the questions people ask right before booking.

Someone searching "rhinoplasty recovery" or "tummy tuck cost near me" is far closer to a consult than someone searching a broad, generic term, so those queries deserve your focus.

</Callout>

Prioritize:

- **Procedure-plus-location** terms that signal local, ready buyers
- **Near-booking questions:** cost, recovery, candidacy, results
- **Surgeon and practice** searches from people already considering you

These convert, even at lower volume.

## <span className="he">📊</span> Why volume seduces and intent pays

Big generic keywords are tempting because the numbers look impressive.

But they bring low intent and brutal competition, often against 20-year incumbents, so they deliver researchers who will never book with you rather than local prospects who will.

Local and procedure-specific terms have less volume but far higher intent and are more winnable, which is why they book more consults per visitor.

## <span className="he">🗺️</span> Map keywords to pages

<Callout type="note">

Research only pays off when it becomes a plan: each meaningful query or tight group gets a page built to answer it.

</Callout>

Most keywords map to a [procedure page](/blog/plastic-surgery-procedure-pages-seo/) or a local page, so the output of good keyword research is a page-and-intent map, not just a list.

The [underlying method](/blog/medspa-keyword-research/) is the same across aesthetics, but for surgeons the emphasis leans hard toward high-intent, winnable local and procedure terms, which is how research turns into [rankings](/blog/how-to-rank-plastic-surgeon-google/) and consults.
