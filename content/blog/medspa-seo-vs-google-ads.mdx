- **Ads rent traffic; SEO builds an asset.** Different tools, different timelines.
- **Ads are fast but stop when you stop paying.**
- **SEO is slow but compounds** into traffic you own.
- **It's rarely either/or.** Sequence them: ads now, SEO underneath.

</AtAGlance>

"SEO vs Google Ads" is framed as a choice, but for most med spas that framing is the mistake.

They solve the same problem, filling your calendar, on completely different timelines, which is exactly why the smart move is usually both, sequenced.

## <span className="he">⚡</span> What ads give you

[Google Ads](/blog/medspa-google-ads/) buy the highest-intent traffic there is, and they do it immediately.

Launch a campaign and you can book consults the same week, which makes paid search unbeatable for filling gaps now or opening a new location.

The catch is that it's rented. The traffic stops the instant you stop paying, so ads are an ongoing cost, not an asset you build.

## <span className="he">🌱</span> What SEO gives you

[SEO](/medspa-seo/) is the opposite trade: slow to start, but it compounds.

It takes months of content, [reviews](/blog/medspa-get-more-reviews/), and local signals before it moves, and then it keeps delivering traffic you don't pay per click for.

<Callout type="key">

Ads are renting traffic. SEO is buying an asset.

A practice that only runs ads restarts from zero every month. One that builds SEO underneath owns a growing share of its bookings.

</Callout>

## <span className="he">🔀</span> The real answer: sequence them

The two aren't rivals, they're phases.

Run ads for immediate flow while SEO builds beneath them, and as your organic traffic grows, shift the mix so a larger share of bookings comes from channels you own and your [acquisition cost](/blog/medspa-customer-acquisition-cost/) falls over time.

Early on, weight toward ads because you need bookings now. Over a year or two, weight toward SEO because it's cheaper and compounding.

## <span className="he">🎯</span> One thing both depend on

Neither lever pays off if your site doesn't convert.

Ads and SEO both just deliver traffic. Whether that traffic books depends on [conversion](/medspa-cro/), so fix that first and every dollar of paid and every hour of organic works harder.
