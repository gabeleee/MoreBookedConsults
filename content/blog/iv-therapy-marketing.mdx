IV therapy is unusual among med spa treatments: patients can come often, which turns it into a recurring-revenue engine rather than an occasional service.

The upside is frequency and flexibility. The catch is a compliance line that's easy to cross.

## <span className="he">📈</span> Demand and frequency

IV therapy's defining trait is how often patients can return, weekly or monthly, which most treatments can't claim.

That frequency makes it a natural [membership](/blog/medspa-membership-programs/) service and gives it more marketing angles than a once-in-a-while treatment, from regulars to one-time event clients.

Demand is steady with bumps around cold season, big events, and post-holiday recovery.

## <span className="he">💰</span> Offer economics: frequency and flexibility

<Callout type="key">

Because patients can come often, IV therapy shines as a membership and add-on treatment.

Memberships for regulars create recurring revenue, [events](/blog/medspa-events-open-houses/) and mobile service extend reach, and pairing drips with other treatments raises per-visit value.

</Callout>

Few treatments give you this many ways to package and sell, which is exactly why IV therapy rewards a deliberate offer strategy.

## <span className="he">⚖️</span> Ad-policy and compliance constraints

<Callout type="warning">

The real risk here is health claims. Overstating what drips do for energy, immunity, or recovery is both a regulatory problem and a trust problem.

Keep marketing language careful and non-guaranteed, and lean on experience and convenience rather than promising medical outcomes. See the [marketing-laws node](/blog/medspa-marketing-laws/).

</Callout>

Stay honest about what the service is, and you avoid the category's main pitfall.

## <span className="he">🎯</span> The conversion angle

Sell ease and routine: a quick, convenient wellness habit that fits a busy life.

Emphasize the experience, the convenience, and the membership value rather than aggressive claims, and let your [page](/medspa-cro/) make booking feel effortless, because for IV therapy, convenience and consistency are what convert and retain.
