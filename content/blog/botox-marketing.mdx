- **Botox is a commodity on price;** win on trust and injector skill instead.
- **Demand is steady year-round,** with mild pre-event bumps.
- **The economics are retention:** patients return every 3–4 months.
- **Ads face injectable restrictions;** lean on reviews, SEO, and referrals.

Every med spa offers Botox, which is exactly why marketing it on price is a losing game.

The practices that win treat Botox not as a transaction but as the start of a relationship that repeats every few months, and they market accordingly.

## <span className="he">📈</span> Demand and seasonality

Botox demand is steady and reliable throughout the year, one of the most consistent in aesthetics.

It gets mild bumps before weddings, holidays, and events, but it doesn't crater in any season, which makes it a dependable revenue base rather than a seasonal spike.

That steadiness is an advantage: you can build predictable retention around it.

## <span className="he">💰</span> Offer economics: think in cycles, not visits

<Callout type="key">

A Botox patient returns every three to four months, so the money isn't in the first visit, it's in the cycle.

Packages, [memberships](/blog/medspa-membership-programs/), and a firm rebooking habit are worth far more than an aggressive per-unit discount that just trains price shopping.

</Callout>

Frame Botox around maintaining a look over time, and structure offers so the second and third visits are built in, because a retained injectable patient has enormous [lifetime value](/blog/medspa-patient-lifetime-value/).

## <span className="he">⚖️</span> Ad-policy constraints

<Callout type="warning">

Injectables face ad restrictions on branded drug names, health claims, and before-and-after imagery, especially on Meta.

You can advertise, but build compliant creative and don't rely on dramatic before-and-afters that platforms will reject. See the [healthcare ad policy node](/blog/medspa-healthcare-ad-policy/).

</Callout>

Because paid creative is constrained, [reviews](/blog/medspa-get-more-reviews/), local SEO, and referrals carry a lot of Botox marketing, and they happen to be exactly what nervous first-time injectable patients look for.

## <span className="he">🎯</span> The conversion angle

First-time Botox patients fear one thing above all: looking frozen or fake.

Lead with natural results, injector expertise, and honest expectations, not price, and let your [page](/medspa-cro/) reassure that specific fear, because trust is what converts an injectable consult, and trust is what brings them back.
