<AtAGlance>

- **Each location needs its own local presence:** page, profile, reviews.
- **The trap is thin, duplicated location pages.** Google ignores them.
- **Per-location profiles and reviews** win more local searches.
- **Consistency and real local substance** are the whole game.

</AtAGlance>

Multi-location and multi-surgeon practices have more to gain from local SEO and more ways to get it wrong, because every location and surgeon multiplies both the opportunity and the risk.

Done right, you win the map in several markets at once. Done wrong, you fragment your signals and rank nowhere well.

## <span className="he">📍</span> Each location earns its own ranking

<Callout type="key">

Google ranks locations, not just practices, so each physical office needs its own genuinely local presence: a distinct location page, its own [Google Business Profile](/blog/plastic-surgery-google-business-profile/), and its own reviews.

You can't win a market you don't have a real local footprint in.

</Callout>

Treat each location as its own local entity, with accurate details and its own [review flow](/blog/plastic-surgery-reviews-realself/), because consolidating everything into one page or profile weakens every location's signals.

## <span className="he">⚠️</span> The thin-location-page trap

<Callout type="warning">

The most common failure is spinning up near-identical location pages, one template with the city name swapped.

Google treats these as thin, low-value content and won't rank them, so they waste effort while looking like progress.

</Callout>

Each location page needs genuinely local substance: the real office, the surgeons there, local details, and information relevant to that market, not a find-and-replace of the last one.

## <span className="he">🔁</span> Consistency across the network

With multiple locations and surgeons, consistency becomes a real project.

Keep names, addresses, and details accurate and consistent across every profile and citation, and manage the [practitioner-vs-practice](/blog/plastic-surgery-google-business-profile/) listing structure deliberately, because duplicate or conflicting listings fragment the signals you're working to build.

The bigger the practice, the more this discipline separates the ones that dominate their markets from the ones that quietly underperform everywhere. It all ties back to the [core SEO plan](/blog/how-to-rank-plastic-surgeon-google/).
