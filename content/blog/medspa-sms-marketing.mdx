<AtAGlance>

- **SMS is the fastest-read channel,** best for time-sensitive nudges.
- **Use it sparingly.** Over-texting gets you muted or reported.
- **TCPA is real:** get consent, honor opt-outs, respect hours.
- **Pair it with email,** which carries the longer message.

</AtAGlance>

A text gets read in minutes, which no other channel can claim, and that makes SMS the sharpest tool a med spa has for anything time-sensitive.

It's also the easiest channel to abuse, both in patience and in law, so the whole game is using it well and using it legally.

## <span className="he">💬</span> What SMS is actually good for

Texting isn't for storytelling. It's for the short, urgent nudge.

- **Appointment reminders** that cut [no-shows](/blog/medspa-no-shows-consult-conversion/).
- **Last-minute openings** when a cancellation leaves a gap today.
- **Short win-backs** to lapsed patients: one line, one reason to return.
- **Confirmations** that reassure and reduce front-desk calls.

Save the longer message for [email](/blog/medspa-email-marketing/), and let SMS do the one thing it does better than anything else.

## <span className="he">⚖️</span> TCPA: the rules you can't skip

<Callout type="warning">

Marketing texts are governed by the TCPA, and violations carry real per-message penalties.

You need prior express consent before you text, a clear opt-out (reply STOP) that you honor immediately, and reasonable sending hours. This is not optional, and "we didn't know" is not a defense.

</Callout>

Practically, that means collecting consent cleanly at booking or intake, keeping records of it, using a platform that manages opt-outs automatically, and never buying lists.

Skim the [marketing-laws node](/blog/medspa-marketing-laws/) before you launch a program.

## <span className="he">🔕</span> Restraint is the strategy

The fastest way to ruin SMS is to over-use it.

Every text spends a little of the patient's patience, so send only when the message is genuinely useful and time-sensitive, and each one lands.

A practice that texts twice a month with real value keeps its audience; one that texts weekly promos gets muted, and muted is worse than unsubscribed because you can't see it.

## <span className="he">🔗</span> SMS and email, together

The strongest programs run both channels in their lanes.

Email carries the sequences and the education; SMS carries the reminder and the urgent opening, and together they cover the full [retention](/blog/medspa-client-retention/) job.
