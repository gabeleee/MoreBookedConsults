Facebook and Instagram work very differently from Google Ads, and treating them the same is why most med spa Meta campaigns disappoint.

Google catches people who are already looking. Meta puts something compelling in front of people who weren't, which is a different job with different rules.

## <span className="he">🌱</span> Meta creates demand, it doesn't capture it

Nobody opens Instagram to buy Botox, so a Meta ad's job is to spark interest, not close a search.

That makes it ideal for showcasing results, introducing a treatment, and floating an [offer](/blog/medspa-intro-offers/) to a well-chosen local audience, filling the top of your funnel with people who'd never have searched yet.

It also means expecting Google-style intent from Meta will always feel like a letdown, because you're comparing two different stages of the buying journey.

## <span className="he">🎨</span> Creative is the whole game

<Callout type="key">

On Meta, the creative is your targeting. A scroll-stopping visual with a clear offer will out-perform any amount of audience tweaking.

Real faces, genuine results, and a specific, believable offer beat polished stock imagery every time.

</Callout>

Lead with the outcome the patient wants, keep it authentic to your practice, and make the next step obvious, because a beautiful ad with no clear action just entertains people.

## <span className="he">⚖️</span> The health-ad rules you have to respect

<Callout type="warning">

Aesthetic and medical advertising falls under stricter policies, and targeting can be limited by special ad category rules.

Before-and-after imagery and targeting on personal attributes are especially restricted. Build your creative and audience strategy around these limits from the start, not after a rejection.

</Callout>

Read the [healthcare ad policy node](/blog/medspa-healthcare-ad-policy/) before you launch, so you don't burn budget on disapproved ads.

## <span className="he">🎯</span> Where Meta really earns its money

For most med spas, the highest return on Meta isn't cold prospecting. It's [retargeting](/blog/medspa-retargeting/).

Showing your results and offers to people who already visited your site or engaged with you converts far better than reaching strangers, so start there and expand outward once it's working.

And send all of it to a page [built to convert](/medspa-cro/), because Meta traffic is cooler than search and needs a stronger landing experience to book.
